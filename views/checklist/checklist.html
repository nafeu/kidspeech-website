What does your infant do before 2 months?
  • Initiates smiling
  • Coos and gurgles
  • Tracks things with eyes & recognizes familiar people
  • Cries to express how they feel
  • Calms in response to gentle touch or caregivers’ voice
  • Smiles in response to high-pitched vocalizations
  • Briefly watches or follows moving objects with eyes
  • Gets startled by loud noises
What does your infant do at 2 months?
  • Makes sounds other than crying
  • Makes differing sounds depending on their mood
  • Initiates smiling
  • Quiets or smiles to a familiar person’s voice
  • Looks at others’ faces
  • Expresses feelings by crying or smiling
  • Relaxes when picked up or spoken to
  • Smiles when others smile at them
  • Recognizes parents
  • Engages in self-soothing strategies such as sucking on their hand
  • Maintains fixation on something (toys) for several seconds
  • Expresses boredom by crying or acting fussy if activity stays the same for an extended period
  • Follows moving object with eyes
  • Becomes interested in new objects
  • Tracks things with eyes and recognizes familiar people
What does your infant do at 3 months?
  • Makes sounds back and forth with you
  • Coos, makes sounds like ooo, aahh, mmmm
  • Turns toward voices or people talking
  • Enjoys physical closeness with caregiver – likes being hugged and cuddled
  • Smiles or gurgles to catch caregivers’ attention
  • Enjoys socializing and playing with others and may cry once play stops
  • Begins to use their memory & display anticipatory behaviours in familiar contexts
  • Follows moving objects with their eyes
  • Reacts to familiar sounds
  • Recognizes loved ones and common objects
What does your infant do at 4 months?
  • Starts to babble with expression
  • Models sounds heard
  • Engages in turn-taking conversations/vocalizations
  • Makes noises to get caregiver’s attention
  • React to toys that make noise
  • Chuckles
  • Smiles spontaneously at people
  • Chuckles when someone tries to get them to laugh
  • Mimics caregivers’ movements and expressions, like smiling and frowning
  • Attentive to the sound of caregivers’ voice
  • Reaches for toys with one hand and brings toys to mouth
  • Increased hand-eye coordination
  • Follows moving objects by moving eyes from side to side
  • Watches faces
  • Recognizes familiar objects and people even if they are in the distance
  • Starts to differentiate between different shades of the same colour
What does your infant do at 6 months?
  • Strings vowels when babbling ‘ah’, ‘oh’
  • Begins producing consonant sounds
  • Makes different sounds for positive and negative emotions
  • Develops a full laugh
  • Blows “raspberries” by sticking out tongue and blowing
  • Displays pleasure while playing with familiar people
  • Knows familiar people
  • Responds to others’ expressions
  • Giggles and laughs
  • Starts to respond to own name
  • Shows curiosity for things and reaches for them
  • Looks at reflection in mirror
  • Reaches to grab toys
  • Becomes fascinated by complex patterns and shapes
  • Aware of the size of objects and adjusts grip accordingly to grasp different sized objects
  • Aware of whether objects are near/far
  • Object permanence abilities begin to emerge
What does your infant do at 9 months?
  • Makes many different sounds like ‘mamamama’ and ‘babababa’
  • Uses finger to point
  • Raises arms to be picked up
  • Understands ‘no’
  • Shy or apprehensive around new people and often gravitates to familiar people
  • Seeks caregiver for comfort, help and play
  • Displays various facial expressions: happy, sad, angry
  • Responsive to own name and makes eye contact if someone calls their name
  • Displays a reaction when caregiver leaves them (looks or reaches for caregiver, cries)
  • Follows the path of an item falling
  • Tries to find objects when dropped out of sight
  • Joint attention skills emerge: Infants look in the same direction as their caregivers and mimic what they’re doing to share the same experience
  • Turns pages in a book
  • Displays signs of curiosity & frequently searches areas of interest
  • Picks up small objects using thumb and pointer finger
  • Identifies the names of some people and objects
What does your child do at 12 months?
  • Uses simple gestures, like shaking head ‘no’, waving ‘bye-bye’, blowing a kiss
  • Sounds have tone changes that resemble speech
  • Says ‘mama’, or ‘dada’ and can express ‘uh-oh’
  • Pays attention and tries to repeat words they hear
  • Copies gestures
  • Expresses needs by using sounds and pointing
  • Comprehends a couple familiar words
  • Likes to play games such as patty-cake and peek-a-boo
  • Gets upset when caregiver leaves and apprehensive around strangers
  • They often play independently and have favourite toys
  • Gives you a book when they want you to read a story to them
  • Begins to pretend-play
  • Play alongside other children but don’t interact directly with them
  • Follows simple requests, ‘pick up the toy’, ‘come here’
  • Recall experiences that occurred a few hours/days prior
  • Explores objects by shaking, dropping, banging or throwing them
  • Able to locate hidden objects
  • Concentrates when being spoken to
What does your child do at 16 months?
  • Says a few words spontaneously
  • Combines sounds and actions (pointing to a cat and saying “ca”)
  • May start putting together two-word phrases
  • Uses gestures (clapping, waving, pointing) to communicate their desires
  • Glances at a familiar object when someone mentions it by name
  • May initiate affection (hugging, kissing, cuddling caregiver)
  • Copies what they see other children doing
  • Experiences separation anxiety when caregiver is not present
  • Mimics caregiver doing household activities
  • Makes eye contact while talking to you
  • Finds simple objects when asked
  • Puts toys in mouth less frequently; plays with toys in hand
  • Able to identify certain objects by name
  • Begins to understand and follow simple one-step commands
  • Points to body parts or pictures in a book when asked
What does your child do at 18 Months?
  • Says at least 3 words other than ‘mama’ or ‘dada’
  • Says and shakes head ‘no’
  • Points to direct attention to something interesting
  • Understands more words than they can speak
  • Can identify some objects by name
  • Poses simple questions by making voice rise at the end of a sentence
  • Able to string two words together
  • Exhibits increased independence and social awareness but still seeks reassurance from caregivers
  • Gets attached to a particular object (blanket or stuffed animal)
  • Doesn’t play directly with other children but are willing to play beside them
  • Likes to show-off their skills to adults, expecting to receive praise
  • Plays pretend
  • Starts to use toys for their intended purposes
  • Understands what simple things are for such as telephone, brush, spoon, TV
  • Follows 2-step verbal commands
  • Willing to try new things if in the presence of familiar adults
  • Helps when getting dressed (eg: pushes arms through sleeve)
  • Can locate an object that they watch being moved from one place to another
  • Have formed a sense of self and able to see themselves as separate from others
  • Show signs of curiosity and likes to grab and move things within reach
  • Refers to self using own name
What does your toddler do at 28 months?
  • Says sentences and phrases using 2 to 4 words
  • Repeats words they hear from conversations
  • Able to use pronouns such as “I”, “me”, “you”
  • Responds to simple questions
  • Enjoys singing parts of songs
  • Displays defiant behaviour
  • Plays mostly beside other children and begins to include other children, such as in chase games
  • May display jealous behaviour
  • Points to things or pictures when they are named
  • Familiar with rhymes in books they know
  • Can follow 2-step verbal commands such as ‘Pick up your toys and put them in the box’.
  • Able to concentrate for longer periods of time (20 minutes if engaged)
  • Can sort things by colour and size
  • They like having control over their experiences and appreciate making small choices or picking between two options

What does your toddler do at 34 months?
  • Starts practicing using manners: “please”, “thank you”
  • Asks “What’s that?”
  • Begins to talk in very short sentences to express their likes and dislikes
  • Knows how to say at least 100 words, including nouns, verbs, adjectives
  • Constructs 3-5 word sentences
  • Becomes highly interested in other children and starts developing friendships but may be bossy during play
  • Gets sensitive/shy in new social situations
  • Practices sharing and taking turns
  • Begins to read how others’ are feeling and provide comfort when they see someone upset
  • Demonstrates simple problem-solving abilities
  • Knows full name
  • Has greater impulse control
  • Can brush teeth and wash hands independently
  • Able to sort objects according to size, shape, pattern and colour
  • Fully comprehends object permanence
  • Builds simple puzzles
  • Understands rules and consequences
  • Thrives on consistent routine
What does your toddler do at 3 years
  • Understands words like ‘behind’, ‘in’, ‘on’, and ‘under’
  • Says words such as ‘I’, ‘me’, ‘we’ and ‘you’ and displays knowledge of plurals (cars, dogs)
  • Can speak using 2-3 short sentences
  • Asks who, what, where, or why questions
  • Uses a vocabulary of more than 500 words and aware of the meaning of more than 3000 words
  • Speaks with a sufficient level of clarity that others can usually understand
  • Engages in short conversations
  • Aware of other children and willingly plays with them
  • Takes turns during play
  • Separates from caregivers with less distress
  • Displays affection towards friends
  • Can copy a circle
  • Follows 2-step and 3-step verbal commands
  • Says first name, age, and gender
  • Able to complete puzzles with 3-4 pieces
  • Uses imagination to come up with stories
  • Understands sentences with two or more ideas: ‘You can have a snack after school’
  • Emerging logic skills & asks ‘why’ frequently
  • Able to adhere to complex instructions
