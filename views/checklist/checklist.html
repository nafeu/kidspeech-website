What does your infant do at 2 months?
  • Initiates smiling
  • Coos and gurgles sounds
  • Tracks things with eyes & recognizes familiar people
What you can do with your 2 month old infant:
  • Initiates smiling
  • Coos and gurgles sounds
  • Tracks things with eyes & recognizes familiar people
Your Infant At 4 Months
  • Starts to babble
  • Babbles with expression and models sounds heard
  • Reaches for a toy
  • Uses hands and eyes together
Your Infant At 6 Months
  • Displays pleasure while playing with familiar people
  • Strings vowels when babbling 'ah', 'oh'
  • Responds to own name
  • Shows curiosity for things and reaches for them
Your Infant At 9 Months
  • Understands 'no'
  • Makes many different sounds like 'mamamama' and 'babababa'
  • Uses finger to point
  • Follows the path of an item falling
  • Likes to play peek-a-boo
Your Child At 1 Year
  • Gives you a book when they want you read a story to them
  • Likes to play games such as 'peek-a-boo' and 'patty-cake'
  • Responds to simple spoken directions
  • Uses simple gestures, likes to shake head to 'no' or waves 'bye-bye', blows a kiss
  • Sounds have tone changes that resemble speech
  • Says 'mama', 'dada' and can express with 'uh-oh!'
  • Pays attention and tries to repeat words they hear
  • Copies gestures
  • Follows simple requests, 'pick up the toy', 'come here'
Your Child At 18 Months (1 1/2 Years)
  • Says many single words
  • Says and shakes head 'no'
  • Points to show what they want
  • Understands what simple things are for such as telephone, brush, spoon, TV
  • Follows 1-step verbal commands without any gestures
Your Toddler At 2 Years
  • Displays defiant behaviour
  • Plays mostly beside other children and begins to include other children, such as in chase games
  • Can point to things or pictures when they are named
  • Says sentences and phrases using 2 to 4 words
  • Can repeat words they hear from conversation
  • Is familiar with rhymes in books they know
  • Can follow 2-step verbal commands such as 'Pick up your toys and put them in the box.'
Your Toddler At 3 Years
  • Follows 2-step and 3-step verbal commands
  • Can name many familiar objects
  • Understands words like 'behind', 'in', 'on', and 'under'
  • Says first name, age, and gender
  • Says words such as 'I', 'me', 'we', and 'you' and displays knowledge of plurals (cars, dogs)
  • Can speak using 2 to 3 short sentences
  • Can copy a circle
