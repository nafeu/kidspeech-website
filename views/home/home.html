COVID-19 Update 2022

Since the start of this global pandemic due to COVID-19, so much has changed. One thing that has not and will not change is our commitment to the children and parents we serve. Our top priority to provide continued Speech and Language Services remains of utmost importance to Kidspeech.

We acted decisively; adhering to all guidelines and directions set forth by provincial and regional health authorities, quickly implementing virtual services (Teletherapy) to all our clients, closing on-site services, following ongoing guidelines, and suspending all groups services to mitigate the spread of the virus and variants.

We all use gloves and perform hand sanitization before and after wearing gloves. We wear masks and face shields and disinfect areas (ie surface of table) and items (ie covers of books, pens) during in-person visits. Physical distancing is a must at all times. Covid screenings are used before each visit. If a client (child or adult) is unwell, the session is aborted. In-person visits occur namely in the community.

As we navigate the pandemic together, Kidspeech continues to monitor and follow advice from the public health authorities.

We encourage parents to call us so we can explain further how virtual services have helped so many of our clients since March 2020. In some cases, it can be beneficial to do in-person in the community.

Certified Life Care Plans

With more than three decades of industry experience, we know how important reliability and credibility are for the insurance industry.

We provide Speech Pathology Independent Examinations (IE), Paper File Reviews (PFR) and Expert Opinions. And certified Life Care Plans (LCP). Our work focuses on cognitive communication skills in children and adults. Our comprehensive reports are of high quality and rely on both qualitative and quantitative data.

The modalities addressed in our reports are: Expressive and Receptive Language skills, Articulation and Phonology, Fluency, Voice, Oral Peripheral Examination, Literacy and early literacy skills, Reading and Writing skills, Swallowing, Digital Literacy, Cognitive Communication (social, pragmatics, executive functions, and memory skills) to name a few areas.

Our Independent Examinations (IE) can be, in some case, be provided virtual so there is no transportation cost to the referral source. In other instances, hen virtual is not an option, we provide services by following strict COVID-19 safety precautions.

We complete a COVID-19 Screener prior to starting the assessment. The assessor wears a mask, gloves and faceshield. Hands are sanitized before and after the session. The areas are sanitized before and after leaving the premises. Physical distancing and wearing PPEs are a must throughout the session.

If the client reports feeling unwell, the session is immediately aborted and the information is reported to the office of the referral source.

We accept referrals for clients (babies, children, teens, adults or seniors) who experience difficulties after a concussion or an acquired brain injury or a spinal cord injury which could have occurred in a car accident, road accident such as while riding a bike, motor bike, ski-doo, diving/ swimming, at birth (negligence), during slips and falls, or in any other circumstances at home, school or in the community (sports).

Our Life Care Plans are often also requested by the legal community for the purpose of setting up a trust for minors or in other circumstances for an adult with a disability.
